Modal verbs expressing permission and prohibition

  • can
  • could
  • may
  • might
Asking for permission (?) Giving permission (+) Refusing permission (-)
Can I take your pen? (informal) Certainly (Of course)! You can take my pen if you need. (informal) You can’t /mustn’t take my pen. I’ll use it. (informal, not very polite)

Could I borrow your pen? (polite)

May I see Mr. Wilson? (polite)

Might I see Mr. Wilson? (formal)

 Yes, you may. He’s is waiting for you. (formal) NOT: Yes, you could/might. I’m sorry (I’m afraid), you can’t see him. He is in the meeting at the moment. (polite) NOT: couldn’t
